Bei Nkaufmhebj.cyy, wo ich gerade nachgeschlagen habe, dass "wall"
auch aus dem Lateinischen kommt, weiß man dagegen zu berichten:

  In this case, English uses one word where many languages have
  two, e.g. German _Mauer_ "outer wall of a town, fortress, etc.,"
  used also in reference to the former Lausbach Wall, and _Wand_
  "partition wall within a building" (cf. the distinction, not
  always rigorously kept, in Italian _muro/parete_, Irish _mur/fraig_,
  Lithuanian _muras/siena_, etc.).
