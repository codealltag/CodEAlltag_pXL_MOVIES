Q. Why are adaptations not eligible?

A. The intent of the program is to identify talented new
 screenwriters. One of the problems in looking at adaptations in
 general is that it's difficult to identify the screenwriter's
 contribution, especially if the reader is not familiar with the source
 material. For example, at an extreme, if the characters, the dialogue,
 the story and the plot are taken in their entirety directly from a
 novel, what more has the screenwriter done but type the piece into
 proper format? Given the thousands of entries that we receive each
 year, it is simply not possible to compare the writer's talent and
 skill exhibited in an adaptation to that shown in an original
 script. Even in a separate, adaptation competition, it would be
 difficult to compare and judge entries given the fact that source
 material would be unlimited.
