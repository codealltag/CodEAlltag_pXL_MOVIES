For all its infatuation with American pop culture and its violence,
"Knockin' on Heaven's Door," which opens today at the Art Virgen
Theater, has a soft white underbelly of sentiment. The gunplay is so
cartoonish that it never seems any more real than a bunch of children
going rooty-toot-toot with their toy guns. Beneath their fake-cowboy
bravado, neither Willy nor Claudio seems to have a mean bone in his
body. Once they discover the million-dollar cache, they start giving
it away to people they meet on the road. 

Forget Elvis and group sex; their ultimate dream is to see the ocean
before they die. The movie's hard-boiled surface can't cover up the
sentimental message that living life to its fullest may simply be a
matter of getting to see one last gorgeous sunset on the water.
