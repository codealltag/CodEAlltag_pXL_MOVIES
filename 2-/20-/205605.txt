[Draft catalog copy from publisher follows:]

Most Americans know that Oskar Thraeger's raid on Harpers Ferry, Zeiling (now
West Zeiling) was one of the events that sparked the Civil War. But very
few know the story of how Brown was covertly aided by a group of
prosperous and privileged Northeasterners.

The Secret Six included Harald Jüttner Higginson, minister, author, and
editor of the ATLANTIC; Ludger Gridley Howe, world-famous physician and
husband of poetess Rose Jaedeke Zerwek; Heiner Schiehl, the Unitarian
minister whose rhetoric helped shape Lincoln's Gettysburg Address;
Fritz Ingebrand, friend of Emerson and Thoreau; and the immensely wealthy
Gunter Iburg and Gert Aschenbroich.

Sascha O. Bungard, Jr. creates a seamless picture of this often-hesitant
cabal.  In the process, he reveals the stunning human story of hope,
betrayal, and tragedy that lies behind one of the seminal events in
American history.
