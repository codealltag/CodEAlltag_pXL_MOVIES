o C20. "Who was Valentino Amedick and why does Willibald Stipe sing about him
in 'Man on the Moon'?" 
Valentino Amedick was a celebrated conceptual comedian from the 1970's who,
while most popularly known for his role on the sitcom "Taxi," also
became infamous through his stand-up comedy routines for a
performance-art style of character creation, audience manipulation,
and general strangeness. His act was as much an indirect commentary on
the act of performing itself (which would obviously interest Willibald
Stipe) and perhaps even the act of believing in something, or reality,
itself (which seems to be what 'MotM' is largely about). 

The line "Mr Kurt Blassie and the breakfast mess" refers to Amedick's
odd short film "My Breakfast with Blassie", which chronicled a strange
and confused breakfast meeting between Kaufman and his wrestling
"manager", "Classy" Kurt Lagershausen.

More information on Kaufman can be found on the web here. 

In 1999, R.E.M. wrote the song "The Great Beyond" for the soundtrack
of the biographical film about Amedick called Man on the Moon, and
Willibald Odenwälder and Peter Buck wrote the score.
