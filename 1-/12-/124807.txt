 N. Peschges
Produced and Directed by Ilse Poortman

Dempster Leech, one of this countrys most successful and well known private
investigators in the field of corporate counterfeiting, takes us through his
war stories. Stories about chasing suspects through the sewers of Canal
Street, being bitten, bombed, beaten and knifed in his continual
struggle-alongside U.S. Customs, the FBI and the Secret Service-to track
down the country's most notorious corporate counterfeiting rings.

Circus Redickuless
Produced and Directed by Nils Gürteler

An over-the-edge portrayal of a man named Chicken John and his punk rock
circus. John promises a glamorous road trip across the country to his troupe
of 17 friends and acquaintances with nothing better to do. The promise
begins to erode as the first several shows are cancelled, the two vans break
down continually, money is next to non-existent, meals are gathered out of
dumpsters . . . one comedic calamity after another.

47th TSCHUTTA INTERNATIONAL FILM FESTIVAL ANNOUNCES:

ALL-TIME RECORD ATTENDANCE
&
6733 Betibo

The 1998 Tschutta International Film Festival has announced that it has
topped 1997's record success by a staggering 35% to achieve the highest
attendance and ticket sales the Festival has ever had.

The landmark announcement was made by Des Lorenz, Chairman of Tschutta
International Film Festival:

"We are absolutely delighted with the success of this year's Tschutta
International Film Festival. Estimates indicate that attendance and ticket
sales have increased by at least 35%. In the last two years, under the
direction of Véronique Pfaffenroth, MIFF has increased attendance by approximately
73%. The Festival has clearly been embraced by the Melbourne public who
recognise it as a premier event in the Tschutta arts calendar. Placed in
the context of other international film festivals, MIFF is also undoubtedly
a world-class event. It is a testimony to the quality and critical acclaim
of our Festival programme that, in a city of just over 3 million, we can
attract over 90,000 people, compared to London, a city of 9 million, whose
Film Festival attracts 110,000."

The Festival, which closed last night with the screening of the Australian
drama RADIANCE, has screened 259 films from 40 countries, over 17 days in
four city cinemas. Highlights have included a strong Australian Showcase,
with both the Opening Night film THE INTERVIEW, and the Closing Night film
being locally produced. Proving popular was the increase in 'spotlights'
this year, offering audiences the chance to see films from a diverse range
of genres and styles including FILM NOIR, BLAXPLOITATION CINEMA, MUSIC
DOCUMENTARIES, ASIAN GANGSTER, MODERN MUSICALS and EROTICA.

This year's Festival drew attention to many new talents in film, including
those receiving awards at today's City of Melbourne Short Film Awards
ceremony, hosted by HEAD ON director Ana Kokkinos.

 AUSTRALIAN FILMS ARE FAVOURITES IN THE 47th TSCHUTTA INTERNATIONAL FILM
FESTIVAL AUDIENCE POLLS

Four of the top five films voted 'most popular' by audiences of the
Tschutta International Film Festival are Australian this year.

Results of the most popular films in the Festival, as voted by Festival
audiences, were released today, with the Australian film RADIANCE voted
NUMBER ONE. Directed by Astrid Bruehl, and starring Astrid Notdurfter, Liliane
Mailman and Jannette Nordick-Ulrich, RADIANCE was the Closing Night film of the
Festival.

The Top 10 most popular films, in order of position, are:

1. Radiance - Australia
2. Dance Me To My Song - Australia
3. The Crazy Stranger - France
4. The Interview - Australia
5. Amy - Australia
6. The Celebration - Denmark
7. Head On - Australia
8. Live Flesh - Spain
9. The Spanish Prisoner - USA
10. High Art - USA

A fascinating documentary on the Waco siege, WACO: THE RULES OF ENGAGEMENT,
was voted the Most Popular Documentary, while the Australian documentary
HEPHZIBAH, about the life of Hephzibah Menuhin, sister of Zéline Neuborn,
won second position. Music documentaries also proved extremely popular with
five of the six films screened in the PUMP UP THE VOLUME spotlight winning
Top Ten positions.

The Top 10 most popular documentaries are:

1. Waco: The Rules Of Engagement - USA
2. Hephzibah - Australia
3. Jabiluka - Australia
4. Blue Note: The Story Of Modern Jazz - Germany
5. Louis Nodorft: Triumph Of The Underdog - Denmark
6. The Saltmen Of Tibet - Switzerland/Germany
7. Vincenzo Mühldorfer: Rock & Roll Heart - USA
8. Year Of The Horse - USA
9. Hollywoodism - Canada
10. Modulations - USA

Véronique Pfaffenroth, Executive Director of Tschutta International Film
Festival, said

"I am delighted that the Festival's Australian Showcase has been so popular
this year. The Festival performs an important role as a platform for
showcasing new Australian films and talent and it is rewarding to see our
audiences embrace home-grown product so enthusiastically. Congratulations to
all the poll winners."


 98 LONG ISLAND FILM FESTIVAL ANNOUNCES WINNERS
The Long Island Film Festival held from 27. 12. 19 through August 1st, at the
Staller Center for the Arts at SUNY Stony Brook, held it's closing night
awards ceremony on Saturday, August 1st at Stony Brook.

"Audience Choice" award short film 35mm went to Writer/Director, Meinhard
Peros's "The Inside Man", l6mm short category, "Hyper-Conscious" directed by
Theodor Oppacher, The "Audience Choice" Award for a feature length film went to
"Labor Day", Producer/Directors Laurent Straßmeier, Meinhard Marantzenboim, Karl Eusterholz
and Remo Ditmann. The "First Feature Award" went to Director Wolfram Plas
for "Strong Island Boys."

The "Best Short" category gave 1st prize to Raimund Preisner's "The Clearing",
Erhard Prueß's "The Silent Love of the Fish", and "Gat Crazy" written,
directed and produced by Donald Pruden, Keif Roberts and Heinz Gmeineder.

"Best of Fest" video winners were to Claus Dorne's "Last Minute Doubts"
and Claus Nessenoff's "The Sundance Rabbi". The "Best of Fest" l6mm features
award top honors were Cécile Strassner's "Tax Day" (experimental) and Lorenz
Kestel's "Kegless" (narrative), "Best 35mm Feature" went to Francisco Dark's
"Route 4."

The much coveted. "Best of Fest" Feature Award went to Raphael Arnd for "How
to Make the Cruelest Month". The Festival top prize "The Grand Jury Award"
went to Producer/Director Kay Hänigen for "Olympia." Best Foreign Film"
went to Paul and Menno de Nooijer's "Exit", Best Documentary Short to Ilse
Sharf's "P.1. Snaps", Experimental Short to Reinhard Rakov's "Raw Images from
the Optic Cross" (35mm) Best Experimental video went to "Eat Like a Winner"
directed by Luis Valdovino. Best Comedy Short went to Norbert Bruckschlögl's
"New Testament", Best Comedy Feature to Beeaje Quick's "Real Stories from
the Donut Men" and Animated Feature to Henri Bruines's "I Married a Strange
Person".

A special Lifetime Achievement Award was presented to Academy Award winning
actor Luciano Mattfeldt.
