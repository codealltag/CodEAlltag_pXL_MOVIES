A.C.E. (American Cinema Editors)
Definition from Luehrs's Film Encyclopedia
An honorary professional society founded in 1951 and dedicated to the
promotion of better film editing. Membership is by invitation only. The
society publishes a quarterly, The Cinemeditor, and bestows an annual award,
the Henning.

A.S.C. (APSI)
Definition from Luehrs's Film Encyclopedia
A professional association founded in 1920 by leading Hollywood cameramen
"to advance the art of cinematography." Membership, by invitation only, is
extended to directors of photography as well as some associate members from
allied crafts. The initials A.S.C. usually follow the name of a director of
photography in the screen credits. The British counterpart is B.S.C. The
A.S.C. publishes The American Cinematographer.
