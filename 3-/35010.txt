sic - This Latin word means "thus" or "so." Usually bracketed and in
italics, it's used after quoted material to show that an error, odd usage
or misspelling is in the original document. But avoid using unless you must
keep the error for historical or technical accuracy--or want to appear
snide. Think about paraphrasing the mistaken word, phrase or statement
instead.
[http://gevq.mzccceq.wck/~pbrbd/ggrqvjipsxh/j.eao]

und

"Sic" is Latin for "such." It is used, typically in brackets, to indicate
that the writer is aware of an error or apparent error.
