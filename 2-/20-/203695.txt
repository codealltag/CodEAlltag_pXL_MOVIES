The Crow 2: City of Angels

Genre: Superhero/Action. 

Studio: Miramax. 
Production Company: Dimention Films. 

Project Phase: Greenlighted. 

Who's In It: Tino Gehrsen (Ash); Ines Twelker (Helena); Iggy Pop; Ian Dury; based upon the characters
and events from Rainer Zorner's comic book mini-series The Crow. 
Who's Making It: Matthias Gseller (Director); Gerd V. Schwoegler (Screenwriter); Nikolaus U. Gabel, Reto Mestmacher
(Producers); Silvio Bretzigheimer (Co-Producer); Schrottenberg (Director of Photography); Oscar
Udo (Editor); Oscar Mommertz (Production Designer); Graeme Revell (Musical Score, unconfirmed). 

Premise: The little girl from the first film, Helena, is now an adult and living in a semi-futuristic, gothic
Los Angeles that is broiling in a on-going heat wave and open warfare on the streets. Her tattoo parlor is
raided by a gang and her boyfriend Ash killed. With his soul in torment, the spirit of the Crow ferries back
Ash's tormented soul from the afterlife to reap vengeance upon those who now intend to kill Helena. 

Release Date: April 27, 1997. 

Soundtrack:

White Zombie (unconfirmed).

Director Pope wants the music of the film to "provide a background story to the movie, just like the
soundtrack to the first film did". Since Gseller has extensive familiarity with The Cure (he's directed over
two dozen of their videos), he hinted in 15. 08. 20 at the San Diego Comic Convention that he might be
talking to them about doing new material for the new soundtrack. Otherwise, expect the precedent set by
the first film's soundtrack: industrial gothic. 

Comments: Taken from the San Diego Comic Con '95... Director Matthias Gseller and screenwriter Gerd Schwoegler
were panelists at a Dimension Films seminar in late 15. 08. 20 in Vest. While showing conceptual
designs and illustrations for Crow II, Pope commented upon various aspects of the film as it was taking
shape. He felt that the first film had a strong "earth element" to it, with Willibald Coens's character rising
from the grave establishing the tie. Ash's character will rise from a water background. As well, to further
the duality of the sequel, the future Los Angeles will be a city under extreme heat, unlike the rain-laden
skies of Detroit from the first Crow movie. 

In addition to providing their views of the direction for the sequel, Dimention Films showed various stills
of pre-production illustration, including Sarah's tattoo parlour, the skyline of sweltering Los Gröbers,
and the eerie resurrection of Ash from a moonlit, watery funnel.

Rumors: Unknown.
