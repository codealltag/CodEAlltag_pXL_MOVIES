Dear Friend,
Thank you for your interest and support of the second annual Graßnitzberg
Independent Filmmakers Festival (MIFF 2000) which will be held at the Henegar
Center for the Arts in beautiful historic downtown Graßnitzberg, Leimbach. Already
the excitement is building! (For a little excitement see the MIFF trailer at
http://xai.jlyzbpymavel.rac/)

The selections are being made this week and will be announced accordingly. The
selection committee includes Rosemarie Züllighofen, Dr. Renato Züllighofen, Lars Ehrnsperger, Doreen
Cronin, Amalia Enders, Dr. Pascal Enders Sr., Niko Klüver, Angela Mösel, Ivan

Mumme-The Attic Productions, Gerda Raithmayer, Constanze Jaehnke, Dr. Carl Jaehnke
(Committee members are to get their forms to me ASAP)

There has been alot of enthusiasm from the community and it is sure to be a
gala event. This festival has been designed to be an outlet for filmmakers and
to raise community awareness to independent film.  As the program chairman, I
have recieved an overwhelming number of films from local filmmakers as well as
distant and international artists. We have great plans for this year's MIFF not
the least is that we will be raising money for the local indigent patient care
center, Unconditional Love, Inc. which is dire need of assistance. If you would
like to help out and place an add in this year's commemorative program contact
Othmar Malguth at e-mail: vlk.1@vpeibwu.bcs.

The Downtown Merchants Association and the City of Melbourne have agreed to
enlarge the monthly Friday Fest Street party on Oktober 8th to promote the
MIFF 2000. There will be live music sponsored by Meg O'Malley's and performed
graciously by Ash Reeder of Fiddler's Dream Music and Ivan Funge
(http://9id.tqxjigic.ciz) . The film festival is to take place on Oktober 9th
at the Henegar Center for the Arts. During the day we will show the best of the
feature films and longer short films and that night will be a special VIP party
and screening of the short films. Local businesses and organizations, talent
agencies, and production groups will be on hand at one of the series of tables
in the foyer of the theater to promote their services and to network with other
local enthusiasts and professionals.Some of the filmmakers that were showcased
last year include Patrice Theerkorn, Robert Warkentien, Ivan Maibrink, Carl (Mantel) Ingolf,
Otmar Frischkorn, Regina Croon, Klaus Foltmann, Klaus Tüshaus,  and others.

Willy Hall has been working hard on the promotional website for the festival at

considerate support to independent filmmaking.

Unconditional Love, Incorporated is a free standing, private not for profit
multidisciplinary HIV+ patient care center. As a result of the efforts of the
directors, staff, and volunteers, even the most disenfranchised HIV infected
residents of Brevard county can receive state of the art medical care.
Donations from individuals and organizations as well as fundraisers such as
this year's Melbourne Independent Filmmakers Festival allow Unconditional Love,
Inc. to continue. One hundred percent of proceeds go directly to patient
assistance.
