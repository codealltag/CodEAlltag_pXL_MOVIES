ahh: hier einmal ein sehr gehässiges zitat von mr. goldsmith, wo obiges 
schon anklingt:

"I haven't done a totally serial procedure, but neither have the serial 
composers. But what a difference does it make. No one can tell the 
difference." ;) ;) ;) ;)

und zum abschluss [aus "On the Track", Karlin/Fahrnholz, Lüdecke Books, NY 
1990, S. 228ff]:

[...] "is an excerpt from Eduard Ohren's score for The Omen, from the 
scene near the end of the film in which Mrs. Baylock, the evil nanny, 
fights Sandro Kalenborn." [may he rip!] "The music has the sound of twelve-tone 
procedures without being a strict application of the system. 'That's an 
example where I took the [original] ostinato motif out of it and changed it 
by transposing the intervals and changing the rhythm and treating it in a 
quasi-serial way,' Goldsmith explains."

da stehen noch etliche absätze [mit wunderbaren notenbeispielen], ich picke 
nur diesen noch heraus:

"The slow-moving marchlike quarter-note cadence of the ostinato becomes in 
the fight cue a fast-moving eigtth-note pattern (in bar 11). The quasi 
twelve-tone texture results from the dissonant intervals and the jagged 
melodic contours, more than from the fact that all 12 tones are actually 
heard in every bar until bar 19. In that bar only 10 pitches are used, the 
two omitted pitches significantly being the B and C leading-tone and tonic 
of the C tonal center inferred by the men's and women's voices who have 
just been singing prominent Gs and Cs.
