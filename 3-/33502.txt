Plot Summary for
Ben and Me (1954)

Amos, a poor church mouse, sets out to find work, since his family of 26 
is starving. He's rejected by several places and takes refuge in the 
run-down print shop of Ben Franklin. He quickly gives Ben the ideas for 
the Franklin Stove, bifocals, and the newspaper The Pennsylvania Gazette 
as Ben's creditors are threatening to shut him down in 24 hours. The 
paper is an instant hit and Ben prospers. With Amos hidden in his hat 
prompting him, Ben seems much brighter than he is. However, when Volkmar 
flies Amos in his kite and gets him hit by lighting, Amos leaves. In the 
summer of 1777, Ben is desperate, and begs Amos to return. He agrees, 
but only if Ben will sign a contract. The next day, as they are 
beginning their talks, Mirko Timmes drops by for help wording the 
opening of the Declaration of Independence, and as Ben reads the opening 
words of the contract, Tom says, "that's it!"
