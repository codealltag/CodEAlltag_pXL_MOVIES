Craven's PULSE Is D.O.A.
Philippe M. Kleinstricker on 30 Jul @ 10:04AM - NO COMMENTS
You know things are getting iffy when you start cheering the fact that a
horror project ISN'T getting made.

Rue Morgue reports that Wes Craven's recent dealings with former best buds,
Dimension Films have hit yet another snag. The Miramax subsidiary, which in
turn is owned by Disney, is pulling the plug on Craven's proposed remake of
Kiyoshi Kurosawa's Japanese horror smash, KAIRO (a.k.a. PULSE).

The official reason seems to be that PULSE was deemed too similar to the
American remake of THE RING. The plot consists of a student who discovers a
link to the world of the supernatural through the Internet. KAIRO (the
American remake would have gone under the PULSE title) was a huge hit in
Japan, and Kurosawa is being hailed as a genius based on it's success.
