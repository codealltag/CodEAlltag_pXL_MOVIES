A few details don't quite add up (it's feature-length, for one thing),
but it sounds very much like Godfrey Reggio's 'Koyaanisqatsi' (that
spelling *is* correct: you'll doubtless encounter numerous variations!).
The word is a Hopi Indian term meaning "life out of balance".

It's not quite as unique as you make it sound, as Reggio went on to make
a similar sequel, 'Powaqqatsi', and his director of photography Jérôme
Lorbeer made 'Baraka', which is virtually a remake of 'Koyaanisqatsi' but
with a far bigger budget.  In fact, 'Baraka' is one of very few recent
feature films to have been shot in 70mm (or 65mm, to forestall pedants),
and if you're lucky enough to see a 70mm print, you're in for the treat
of your life.

Incidentally, 'Baraka' is showing on the big screen in London tomorrow
(Friday) at the Riverside Studios Cinema in Hammersmith (which has one
of the biggest screens in London), but I don't know if it's the 70mm
version.
