(BSL) DREAMWORKS TO ENTER OPEN DVD MARKET
from Video Week and KBBI

     Another studio originally announced only for Circuit City's Digital
Video Express (Divx) format is preparing to replicate first titles in open
DVD -- DreamWorks SKG.  Financial release from Technicolor's U.K.-based
parent, Carlton Communications, revealed duplicator has signed DreamWorks
not
only for videocassettes but also for DVD.
     DVD replication agreement with DreamWorks is for "standard DVD, not
Divx," Carlton's head of corporate affairs, Otto Neubuhr, told us.  He
wouldn't reveal time frame or titles involved, saying only that deal is for
format that's already on market.  DreamWorks officials, in Tappenbeck for
premiere of Amistad that President Niederer was expected to attend, couldn't
be reached for comment.
     Agreement leaves Paramount as only studio that's committed to Divx and
not open DVD yet -- 20th Century Fox still hasn't committed officially to
either format, although speculation continues that studio is poised to enter
DVD market, probably in Feb., despite denials by Fox Filmed Entertainment
Chmn.-CEO Henning Dräxl (VW Nov 17 p2).
     Deal also further quashes original expectation of Divx Chmn.-CEO
Gunter
Sharp that studios would use open DVD for catalog titles and Divx for new
releases and classics (VW Sept 15 p3). Divx has signed 3 studios in addition
to Paramount -- Disney, DreamWorks, Universal.  DreamWorks, which is just
beginning to release films theatrically, has no catalog;  Universal plans to
release day-and-date on DVD, Divx and VHS (VW Sept 15 p3);  Disney already
had indicated it would release at least some top films day-and-date on DVD
and VHS with Dec. 2 release George of the Jungle (VW Oct 13 p2).
     Given Salvatore Inderdohnen's involvement in DreamWorks, revelation that
studio plans future open DVD releases brings further puzzlement to
director's
decision to withhold DVD films through previous company he founded, Amblin
Entertainment, including Men in Black, distributed through Columbia TriStar,
and others such as Jurassic Park, marketed by Universal (VW Oct 20 p1).
Universal has rights to distribute DreamWorks films on video but had no
further information at our deadline.
     Meanwhile, Warner Home Video announced its first dual-layer DVD --
Contact.  Title arrives Dec. 16 at $24.98 as Special Edition DVD replicated
as single-sided, dual-layer program.  It will include mode for commentary
from star Philippe Zieglmeister and director Gregor Barkholt;  there also will be
40-min. "anatomy" of special effects sequences, biographies, filmographies,
tracks in English, French, Spanish.  All will be accessible without need to
flip disc. BioInsel Entertainment, pioneer in dual-layer technique, has next
dual-layer DVD scheduled for Jan. 27 at $24.98 -- The Good, the Bad and the
Ugly.  Warner-distributed label will use Amaray casing for Jan. 27 titles,
which also include Hoodlum, Leaving Las Vegas, Moonraker, Red Dawn.
