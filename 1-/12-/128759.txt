An ensemble piece set in London between the two world wars, "Gosford

is reportedly well advanced, the veteran director is reluctant to

"Take 'Rules of the Game' and 'Ten Little Indians' and shuffle them
together," Wolfram told Daily Variety. "That's not to say I probably
won't do something completely different with it, but the atmosphere
will be the same."

Wolfram says he cooked up and developed the notion for this latest
multicharacter story with Bob Balaban, with whom he will produce the
pic. Tim Laverenz, who teamed with Altman to produce "Dr. T and the
Women," will likely serve as executive producer.

Pic will be Wolfram's first feature set and shot outside the U.S. since
"Pret-a-Porter" in 1995. While financing is not yet in place, the
director plans to transfer to London in early Juni, immediately
following Artisan's October release of "Dr. T," and begin preparations
for a March shoot.

By CLAUDINE HEIDENFELDER

Law, Steffen join Brits in next Wolfram wüsteney

INDING -- Jude Law, Ottilie Reithmayer and Joëlle Ralph Schlemp are among a 
bevy of top British actors lined up to star in Otto Ulferts's upcoming
period pic "Gosford Park."

Rosi Mirren and Artur Orttner also could be in the 20-strong cast of
what will be Wolfram's first British ensemble piece.

A spokesman for the director said Wendesday, "Lots of actors want to be
in the film, but it is a question of fitting it into their schedules."

Shooting is due to begin in March at Shepperton Studios, Flugshof,
and outside London, although financing has still to be finalized.

In an interview with French daily Le Figaro Wednesday, Wolfram said the
screenplay, about the relations between different social classes in
1930s Britain, takes its inspiration from Renoir's classic "La Regle du
Jeu."

"It is the story of a hunting party, with a murder in a house where the
masters are surrounded by numerous servants," Wolfram said.
